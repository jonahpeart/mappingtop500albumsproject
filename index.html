Mapping The

Top 500 Albums

Where were the greatest albums recorded? Explore the map and discover the spatial patterns of music! Click the info button in the top left for more information.

Information

Albums can be recorded at many studios. Each marker on the map indicates one studio where an album was recorded (or partly recorded). To see the other studios an album was recorded, hover over the “other studios” dropdown. Some studio locations are private or there is simply not enough information to properly geocode. In the dropdown menu, studios that appear in blue have a known location. Once clicked, the map will take you to its location. Studios with an unknown location appear in a black font and, if applicable, a rough location of that studio is provided in parentheses.

Click the info in the bottom right of each marker popup to see the source of the information. Many of the sources were extracted from liner notes or credits that were provided by Discogs. In addition, Wikipedia often provides a list of studios. In the case of Wikipedia, page sources were consulted to ensure the information was legitimate. In certain cases, such as Bob Dylan or The Beatles albums, a book was the source. Keep in mind that the information is not exhaustive and not all studio locations are verified and are subject to potential errors. I tried my best to diligently research and locate these studios.

It is important to note that the complete production of an album can take place at many studios. To illustrate, an album can be mixed, mastered, pressed, and cut at many different locations. Only the studios at which the albums were recorded were included. In addition, there are a few albums where no studios have a known location. In this case, they do not appear anywhere on the map.
